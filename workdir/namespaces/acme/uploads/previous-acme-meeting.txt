--- Section 2: Technical Deep-Dive ---

INFORMATICA SITUATION (James, Priya):
- Currently running 617 active SSIS packages. Priya estimates roughly 200 of them are "zombie" packages — scheduled but no downstream consumer in the past 12 months.
- The Informatica PowerCenter server is running on a physical box that is 8 years old. No virtualisation. Hardware failure is a real risk.
- James said: "We had a scare in November — the box went down for 4 hours and we couldn't do month-end payroll accruals. That's what got the CFO to sign off on this budget."
- They have no documentation for ~60% of the packages. Tribal knowledge only.

CLOUD STRATEGY:
- Sarah confirmed they have an Azure EA but are "not religious about it". She said they looked at Snowflake last year but shelved it because "the bill shocked us in the POC".
- They are open to Databricks (already have a workspace) but James is concerned about vendor lock-in.
- Our SA asked about Iceberg vs Delta Lake preference — James said the ML team prefers Delta Lake because they're already on Databricks, but he personally wants "the most portable option". No firm preference yet.
- AWS: minimal footprint, no desire to expand. Azure is the primary cloud.

GOVERNANCE:
- Collibra licence confirmed: Enterprise tier, ~$220k/year, renewed Jan 2026. Currently used by 2 people. James described it as "expensive shelfware". He is open to getting value from it but not willing to bet the project on it.
- The data governance analyst is part-time and reports to compliance, not engineering. There's internal politics here — Sarah hinted that compliance wants "ownership" of governance but doesn't want to resource it properly.
- No data quality framework exists. Issues are caught reactively (usually by downstream consumers complaining).

METRIC INCONSISTENCY (Sarah, explicitly frustrated):
- The CFO gets three different "loan book" numbers depending on which dashboard he looks at. This is the #1 business pain point.
- The discrepancy between BU definitions has been known for 2 years. Two previous attempts to create a "golden record" failed — both times due to political disagreement between business unit heads over who owns the definition.
- Sarah said: "I need a vendor who can be the honest broker here. We can't resolve this internally."
- This is a POLITICAL problem as much as a technical one. Our proposal needs to acknowledge this explicitly.

ML TEAM FRICTION:
- Priya runs point on data engineering for the ML team. She said the biggest blocker is feature pipelines — currently every new feature requires a DBA to write a stored proc and a manual SSIS package.
- The ML team has been using dbt for one domain (retail scoring) for 6 months. Priya loves it: "If we had dbt across the board and a proper scheduler, my team would be 3x faster."
- She specifically asked about our experience with dbt + Airflow + Databricks as a stack. We confirmed experience. This landed well.

REGULATORY REPORTING:
- CECL provisioning is done quarterly. Currently a 3-person manual process with Excel. The most recent CECL run took 11 days from data freeze to sign-off. Auditors flagged it as a control weakness.
- DFAST (stress testing) is annual. The data team spends 6 weeks on data preparation alone.
- Opportunity: automate the data preparation layer for both. Not necessarily the models themselves, but the data extraction, validation, and formatting.

TEAM CAPABILITIES:
- James was candid: "My team is SQL Server engineers. They're good, but they don't know Spark and they've never worked cloud-native." Training is a must-have, not a nice-to-have.
- They want embedded training during Phase 3, not a separate workshop at the end.
- Sarah asked if we could commit to pairing our engineers with their engineers on each pipeline build. We said yes, and this is now a COMMITMENT we need to reflect in the proposal.

--- Section 3: CFO Session (Derek Hollis, 11:45–12:15) ---

Derek joined specifically for the commercial discussion. Key points:

BUDGET:
- The $1.2M–$1.5M envelope is approved. He said "up to $1.5M if the business case is clear — we're not counting pennies here but I don't want surprises."
- He asked directly about our day rates. We gave a range ($2,000–$2,800/day depending on role). He didn't flinch but asked about fixed-price options for Phase 1 and Phase 2.
- He prefers fixed-price for the assessment and design phases ("I want to know what I'm getting"), then T&M with a cap for the implementation phases.

ROI EXPECTATIONS:
- Derek is tracking 3 business outcomes:
  1. Month-end close: reduce from 9 days to 5 days within 12 months of go-live
  2. Compliance cost: reduce manual data reconciliation from ~30% of risk team FTE to <10%
  3. ML deployment speed: reduce model iteration cycle from 6 weeks to 2 weeks
- He asked if we could commit to these in the proposal. We said we'd frame them as "target outcomes" rather than contractual guarantees, and he accepted that.

PAYMENT TERMS:
- He expects monthly invoicing aligned to milestones. Milestone sign-off by James and Sarah.
- He will push back on any payment terms longer than Net-30.
- He mentioned the 5% penalty clause in the RFP and said "that's standard, we enforce it".

KEY THEMES FOR PROPOSAL

1. Speed and pragmatism — Sarah does not want theory. Lead with working deliverables.
2. Political broker — metric inconsistency is a political problem. Position us as a neutral third party with a process for resolving definition conflicts.
3. Paired delivery / knowledge transfer — embedded training, not a workshop.
4. Fixed-price Phase 1+2, T&M with cap for Phase 3+4.
5. Collibra activation — they're paying for it, they're not using it. Offer a quick win here.
6. dbt + Airflow + Databricks — Priya already sold internally on this stack. Align to it.
7. ROI anchoring — Month-end from 9→5 days, reconciliation from 30%→10% FTE, ML cycle 6 weeks→2 weeks.
8. Regulatory reporting — CECL and DFAST data prep automation is a high-value quick win.
9. Risk: Informatica box failure — lead with risk mitigation, not feature delivery.
10. Competition: Deloitte likely on price (high) and brand. Boutique likely on price (low) and flexibility. We win on delivery credibility and FS depth.
